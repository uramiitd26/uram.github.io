Urban and Regional Air Quality Management

Researching cleaner air for healthier cities.

The Urban and Regional Air quality Management (URAM) group at IIT Delhi focuses on regional and urban-scale chemical transport modeling, while working closely with regulatory bodies in India and abroad to shape practical air-quality action plans.

Explore research Contact the group
  • Focus Regional and urban air-quality modeling
  • Approach Research aligned with policy and action planning
  • Base Department of Civil Engineering, IIT Delhi

Research Interests

Science, modeling, and actionable insight.

Our work spans the formation, transformation, and chemical mechanisms of air pollutants near their sources. We build and improve air-quality models that help explain current conditions and support better environmental planning.

The group also studies source apportionment, indoor air quality, regional air quality, and air purification, connecting computational analysis with real-world urban challenges.

  • Formation and transformation of air pollutants near sources
  • Development of air-quality models
  • Source apportionment of air pollutants
  • Indoor and regional air-quality studies
  • Air purification research
  • Policy-facing environmental action support

Leadership

Dr. Sri Harsha Kota

Group Leader

Dr. Sri Harsha Kota completed his Bachelor of Engineering in Civil Engineering from CBIT, Osmania University, Hyderabad, India in 2007. He received his Master of Science and Doctoral degrees from Texas A&M University, College Station, USA in 2014.

He worked as an Assistant Professor at IIT Guwahati for more than three years before joining IIT Delhi in May 2018. He is currently an Associate Professor in the Department of Civil Engineering at IIT Delhi.
